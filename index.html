Strategy
Smart Preparation Guide
Proven strategies to crack competitive exams faster and smarter.
📅
Make a Study Timetable
  • Study 6–8 hours daily minimum
  • Give 2 hours to each subject
  • Keep Sunday for revision only
  • Take 10 min break every hour
  • Stick to same schedule daily
📝
Previous Year Papers
  • Solve last 10 years of papers
  • Understand question patterns
  • Note frequently repeated topics
  • Practice under strict time limits
  • Analyze mistakes after each paper
📰
Current Affairs Daily
  • Read The Hindu or Indian Express
  • Watch DD News for 30 minutes
  • Make concise daily notes
  • Focus on government schemes
  • Track awards & appointments
🧠
Smart Revision Technique
  • Use spaced repetition method
  • Revise after 1 day, 1 week, 1 month
  • Create mind maps for topics
  • Teach others what you learn
  • Write from memory without notes
✍️
Answer Writing (UPSC)
  • Intro → Body → Conclusion structure
  • Use diagrams and flowcharts
  • Write in points, not paragraphs
  • Give examples from current affairs
  • Practice 2 answers daily minimum
🎯
Exam Day Strategy
  • Sleep well the night before exam
  • Attempt easy questions first
  • Skip and return to tough ones
  • Watch time every 15 minutes
  • Never skip questions you know

Frequently Asked Questions
Best government exam after 12th?

NDA (Defence), SSC CHSL (Clerical), RRB Group D (Railways) are great options. For professional courses: JEE Main (Engineering) and NEET UG (Medical) are top picks after 12th with PCM/PCB.

How many UPSC CSE attempts are allowed?

General: 6 attempts (up to age 32). OBC: 9 attempts (up to 35). SC/ST: unlimited (up to 37). EWS: 6 attempts (up to 32). PWD candidates get additional relaxation.

Can I apply for JEE Main and Advanced same year?

Yes. First qualify JEE Main and rank in the top 2.5 lakh to become eligible for JEE Advanced. Both exams occur in the same academic year (January–May).

Is NEET mandatory for all medical colleges?

Yes. Since 2020, NEET UG is the single national entrance for MBBS, BDS, and Ayush admissions in all government and private medical colleges across India.

SSC CGL or IBPS PO — which is better?

SSC CGL offers more posts across central government (14,000+ vacancies) while IBPS PO offers slightly better pay (₹52,000) and banking growth. Choose based on your interest — government departments vs banking sector.
